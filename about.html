Writer & Reader of Feelings

Chada Montsho

I started Yearning Pages because I've always found comfort in books that understand the harder feelings—the melancholy, the longing, the quiet moments of solitude that speak volumes.

As someone who writes poetry and studies psychology, I've learned that the feelings we're told to hide—anxious attachment, avoidance, the ache of yearning—are often the ones that connect us most deeply to each other and to art.

Why "Yearning Pages"?
"Yearning is that space between wanting and having—the ache of almost, the beauty of not yet. Every great book lives in that space."
